01About关于

My research interests include causal inference methods, sexuality and gender, mental and behavioral health, and integrative medicine. 我的研究兴趣包括：因果推断方法、性与性别研究、心理与精神卫生、整合医学。

I am a PhD candidate in Epidemiology and Population Health at Stanford University School of Medicine, advised by Dr. David Rehkopf. My training bridges clinical medicine (MBBS) and population health (MPH, Harvard T.H. Chan). 我目前在斯坦福大学医学院攻读流行病学与人群健康博士，导师是 David Rehkopf 教授。我的学术背景横跨临床医学（MBBS）与人群健康（哈佛陈曾熙公共卫生学院 MPH）。

I work on three questions: What causes disease? How do we understand human sexuality and gender? And how can we make populations healthier? I trained in clinical medicine and traditional Chinese medicine at Shanghai Jiao Tong University and Shanghai University of Traditional Chinese Medicine, and in epidemiology at the Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health and Stanford. 我尝试回答：疾病发生的原因是什么？人类的性与性别是怎样的现象？我们如何让人口更健康？我先后在上海交通大学与上海中医药大学接受临床医学与中医学的训练，在哈佛陈曾熙公共卫生学院与斯坦福大学接受流行病学的训练。

    China's 2017 Censorship Policy and Attitudes Towards Same-Sex Behavior: An Interrupted Time Series Analysis中国 2017 年审查政策与同性行为态度：一项中断时间序列分析

    Lu J, Rehkopf DH, Bendavid E.

    Did China's 2017 regulation restricting media portrayals of sexual and gender minorities shift how people feel about same-sex behavior? Using interrupted time series analysis on seven waves of the Chinese General Social Survey, we find the policy was followed by a sharp near-term drop in positive attitudes — sharpest among men — and diverging longer-term trajectories between frequent and infrequent internet users.中国 2017 年限制性少数群体媒体呈现的审查政策，是否改变了公众对同性行为的态度？我们用中国综合社会调查（CGSS）七轮数据做了中断时间序列分析，发现政策出台后正面态度短期内显著下降（男性降幅更大），而从长期看，频繁使用互联网与较少使用互联网的人群态度走势发生了明显分化。

Using linked electronic health records from All of Us, we found sexual and gender minority adults had markedly elevated odds of bipolar disorder, PTSD, depression, and ADHD — disparities we interpret as the footprint of stigma and minority stress, not identity.截至目前，这是规模最大的一项全美国性研究，归纳了性取向与性别认同与精神健康的关系。基于 All of Us 队列的电子病历数据，我们发现性少数人群出现双相障碍、创伤后应激障碍、拑郁和注意力缺陷多动障碍的几率明显升高。我们认为，这种差异反映的是社会污名和少数压力，而不是身份本身。
